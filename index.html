02 / 11 The Problem

Three teams. Zero shared truth.

Customer Success
Managing hundreds of individual client project forecasts (worth upwards of $50M each!) in unversioned Google Sheets — no audit trail, anyone could edit at any time, no history of what changed or why.
Operations
Capacity models running in Excel files that routinely crashed, blocking global resource deployment. No visibility into SLA pacing or where to prioritize fulfillment.
Finance
No reliable data for headcount planning or margin modeling. Billing required 50+ people manually reconciling contracts — and the org regularly over- and under-billed clients.
Root cause: All three functions were tightly coupled operationally but had no shared data layer — changes in one team were invisible to the others until things broke.
03 / 11 The Core Gap

The absence of a single source of truth

Why this was the #1 problem
Of all the dysfunction created by the fragmented tooling, the absence of a single source of truth was the most costly and the hardest to fix. Without it, every team was operating from a different version of reality — and everyone knew it. Senior leaders spent more than 10 hours per month in meetings just trying to determine what was actually real: which forecast numbers to believe, which assumptions had changed, and who had made the last update. None of this was tracked systematically, so every planning cycle started from scratch.

On top of that, a team of people spent over 100 combined hours every month manually pulling data from disparate sources, compiling it, and computing the outputs needed just to walk into those meetings. This project eliminated both entirely — changes in assumptions, forecast updates, and pipeline shifts were captured automatically and published daily. The SSOT tracked over $1B in project value across the CS pipeline.

04 / 11 CRM Foundation

Building clean data in at the source

A SSOT is only as good as the data flowing into it. The Salesforce build was designed around one principle: make the right thing easier than the wrong thing. Rather than relying on reps to follow process, the system guided them through it. A network of coordination flows, modular subflows for efficient processing, and screen flows that kept users on a clear path meant that the experience felt natural — even to team members who had never used Salesforce before. This is what allowed a 50-person team to be fully onboarded and operational in 90 days.

Screen Flows
Guided user experience from day one
Core CS workflows were built as screen flows — step-by-step guided interfaces that kept users on track, surfaced the right fields at the right time, and prevented incomplete data entry. For a team with no prior Salesforce experience, this made onboarding dramatically faster and reduced support overhead to near zero. The flow, not the user, enforced process.
Screen flows Guided data entry Fast onboarding
Flow Architecture
Coordination flows with reusable subflows
Complex automations were structured as parent coordination flows calling standalone subflows — each subflow handling a discrete, testable piece of logic. This modular design made the system easier to maintain, extend, and debug. Subflows for contract calculations, capacity notifications, and data validation could be updated independently without rebuilding entire processes.
Modular subflows Reusable logic Maintainable architecture
Data Automation
Derived fields replace manual entry
Revenue projections, consumption forecasts, and pacing metrics were calculated automatically from structured inputs. Formula fields and triggered flows kept derived values in sync the moment upstream data changed — removing a class of errors that previously caused multi-million dollar billing discrepancies.
Formula fields Triggered flows Auto-calculated ARR
Pipeline Automation
Stage transitions trigger downstream updates
When a deal advanced in Salesforce, automated flows triggered capacity record updates, Ops team notifications via Slack, and structured data pushes to the Snowflake pipeline. What previously required a rep to manually email Ops and update three separate tools happened automatically — consistently and without human intervention.
Stage-triggered flows Slack notifications Snowflake pipeline sync
Design principle
Every automation removed a manual step. Every screen flow removed a decision. Every subflow encapsulated a rule that previously lived in someone's head. By the time data reached Snowflake, it had already been validated, structured, and enriched at the source — by the system, not the user.
05 / 11 SLA Attainment & Pacing

Turning delivery data into operational decisions

Customer contracts included SLAs with both incentive bonuses for over-delivery and financial penalties for falling short — making pacing visibility not just operationally important, but directly tied to revenue. Without a systematic view of which projects were ahead or behind expected pace, the operations team was flying blind. Fulfillment was prioritized reactively, penalties were discovered after the fact, and incentives were routinely left uncaptured.

Incentives
Captured proactively by surfacing ahead-of-pace projects that could be accelerated for bonus attainment
Penalties
Reduced by identifying at-risk projects early enough for Ops to intervene before SLA thresholds were breached
Prioritization
Ops team received a dynamically updated fulfillment queue ranked by SLA urgency and incentive opportunity

Ops team impact
Rather than reacting to missed SLAs after the fact, the operations team had a daily prioritization view. At-risk projects were surfaced early, with enough lead time to reallocate resources and course-correct before penalty thresholds were crossed.
CS team impact
CS managers had full visibility into their book of business — which clients were at risk, which were ahead of schedule, and what the financial implications were. This changed conversations with clients from reactive to proactive.
Finance team impact
SLA attainment data fed directly into the billing model — incentive credits and penalty deductions were calculated systematically rather than reconciled manually, eliminating a major source of billing errors.
How changes were communicated
Pacing updates and SLA threshold alerts were surfaced through dedicated Slack channels for each team and through role-specific BI dashboards in Sigma — delivering the right information to the right person without requiring them to log in and check.
06 / 11 Billing Automation

From 50-person manual reconciliation to fully automated invoicing

Before this system existed, billing required a team of 50+ people to manually review contracts, cross-reference delivery data, and reconcile invoices — every billing cycle. Customer contracts were consumption-based with a wide variety of escalators, exclusion clauses, SLA incentive credits, and penalty deductions. None of this was systematically tracked anywhere. As a result, the business regularly over- and under-billed clients — creating both revenue leakage and client relationship risk at scale.

Contract source of truth
All contracts, pricing tiers, escalator schedules, exclusion clauses, and amendment history were loaded into Snowflake as structured data — for the first time in company history. CS managed this directly, keeping it current for new clients and amendments.
SLA adjustments built in
Incentive credits for over-delivery and penalty deductions for SLA shortfalls were calculated automatically from the pacing model and applied to invoices systematically — no manual review required.
NetSuite automation
Using the contract SSOT and delivery data in Snowflake, invoices were generated and pushed directly into NetSuite. What previously consumed an entire team's billing cycle became a scheduled automated process.
Audit & compliance
An approval workflow and field-level audit trail were designed in partnership with the compliance team — tracking every material change to billing data with user attribution and timestamps. The full system was reviewed and approved by third-party auditors as part of the company's IPO readiness process.

09 / 11 Delivery

How it was built

Phase 1
Salesforce CRM — 90 days
Stood up Salesforce for the 50-person CS team with screen flows, modular subflows, and enforced data structures. A team with no prior Salesforce experience was fully operational in 90 days. Google Sheets were retired.
Phase 2
SSOT — data modeling & BI
Migrated all forecasting logic into dbt models in Snowflake. Built role-specific Sigma dashboards and Slack alerting for CS, Ops, and Finance — with change history, daily snapshots, and root cause attribution. Eliminated 100+ hours/month of manual data work.
Phase 3
SLA tracking & Ops prioritization
Built pacing models comparing actual delivery against expected pace for every active project. Created a dynamic Ops fulfillment queue surfacing at-risk and incentive-eligible projects — directly improving SLA attainment and shifting the team from reactive to proactive.
Phase 4
Automated billing system
Built a contract and pricing SSOT covering all consumption-based agreements, escalators, exclusions, and SLA adjustments. Automated invoice generation into NetSuite, replacing a 50+ person manual reconciliation process.
Phase 5
Audit & IPO readiness
Designed a full approval workflow and field-level audit trail for all billing data — with user attribution and timestamps on every material change. Reviewed and approved by third-party auditors in preparation for public company standards.
